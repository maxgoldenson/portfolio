I'm a robotics and mechatronics engineer pursuing dual degrees in Mechanical and Electrical Engineering at CU Boulder. My work sits at the intersection of precision mechanisms, embedded electronics, sensors, and control systems.

At Brewbird, I've worked on every layer of a commercially deployed smart coffee machine — from redesigning load-bearing mechanisms and developing novel encoder technology to building automated test fixtures that surface hidden failure modes. I thrive on problems that don't have off-the-shelf solutions.

My personal projects reflect the same drive: a custom delta 3D printer iterated through five major revisions, a sub-$5 linear encoder accurate to ±10μm, smart food-waste-reducing tongs with a custom PCB, and deep dives into FOC motor control from first principles.

Outside the lab, I've led and mentored a 25-person competitive robotics team, competed in FRC at the national level, and built everything from electric skateboards to onewheel clones. I believe the best engineers build things — and then iterate until they actually work.
