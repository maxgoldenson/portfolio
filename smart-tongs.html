Smart Tongs — Food Waste Reduction

A fully custom ESP32 IoT device — with a bespoke PCB, precision encoder, and wireless connectivity — designed to reduce student food waste by making consumption visible in real time. Won a sustainability award.

01 — Problem

Students over-serve. No one notices until it's too late.

Food waste in cafeteria settings is largely driven by oversized initial servings — students take more than they need before realizing it. The problem isn't awareness; it's that there's no real-time feedback at the point of serving to nudge behavior.

Existing solutions (signage, portion guides) are passive and ignored. We needed something embedded in the act of serving itself — unobtrusive, immediate, and quantifiable.

"Students need a way to be mindful of their consumption habits — at the moment it matters, not after."

02 — Process

System design from hardware to wireless

As the electronics lead, I owned the full hardware stack: sensor selection, schematic capture, PCB layout, firmware, and power management. The mechanical team handled the tong body and integration.

Serving measurement
Magnetic quadrature encoder on the tong hinge, accurate to 0.1°. Combined with an MPU-6050 IMU to distinguish a deliberate serving motion from idle handling.
Custom PCB (TEU)
Designed the Tong Electronics Unit from scratch in EasyEDA — dual-core ESP32-S3, battery management IC, auto wake-up circuit, and all peripheral connections on a board sized to fit inside the tong handle.
Power management
30-day battery life achieved via aggressive sleep modes and a motion-triggered wake circuit fed by the IMU. Smart voltage monitoring and temperature-based shutoff protect the 2200mAh Li-ion cell.
User feedback
OLED display shows current and cumulative serving data. Rumble motor and piezo buzzer provide haptic and audio cues when a threshold is exceeded — immediate, non-disruptive feedback.

03 — Solution

A fully integrated smart serving device

The final device looks and feels like a normal pair of tongs. The TEU PCB is hidden inside the handle, the encoder is embedded in the hinge axle, and the OLED is visible on one arm. Wireless data relay to a base station via WiFi (BLE also supported) enables aggregate reporting without any wired connection.

The system is designed for deployment at scale — long battery life, durable construction, and over-the-air configurability mean a cafeteria could run a fleet of these without per-meal intervention.
